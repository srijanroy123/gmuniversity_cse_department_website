Laboratory Summary

The Department of Computer Science & Engineering at GM University maintains well-equipped laboratories that form the foundation for academic instruction, research, and innovation. With over 180 computing systems, the department balances modern high-performance machines and legacy systems to cater to a wide spectrum of courses, from programming fundamentals to advanced domains like Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, Cloud Computing, and Cybersecurity. Recent additions include I-Active All-in-One systems with 12th Gen Intel Core i5 processors, 16GB RAM, and NVMe SSD storage, providing students with industry-ready tools for hands-on learning.

The department actively encourages a student-driven model of innovation, inviting learners to submit proposals for the purchase of additional equipment, accessories, and specialized hardware needed for their projects. These purchases not only support immediate academic requirements but also add lasting value to the labs, benefiting future cohorts. Faculty members and students collaborate closely on these initiatives, ensuring that laboratory upgrades reflect both academic goals and real-world applications.

In addition to routine upgrades aligned with AICTE norms and ACM curriculum guidelines, the department regularly forwards proposals for infrastructural enhancement, benchmarking itself against leading institutions. To further support innovation and self-paced learning, labs are kept open beyond scheduled hours, enabling students to engage in hackathons, coding practice, project development, and research activities without restrictions.
