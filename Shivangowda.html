Profile

Dr. Shivanagowda G. M. is a Professor and Head of the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at GM University, Davanagere. He joined GMU on October 18, 2023, bringing with him nearly two decades of academic, research, and administrative experience from SDM College of Engineering & Technology (SDMCET), Dharwad.

At GMU, he also serves as the Associate Director of ERP, leading the design and development of in-house enterprise systems to streamline academic and administrative processes. As the Scholarship Nodal Officer, he ensures transparency and efficiency in student financial support from the Government and NGOs. Additionally, he is the Editor of GMU Times, the university's quarterly campus magazine, which highlights academic, cultural, and research activities.

Dr. Shivanagowda is also actively engaged in critical academic administration areas such as USN coding, subject coding, and employee data management, which he has been overseeing since the very inception of GM University, as these are vital for aligning university operations with national-level education frameworks and ensuring smooth administrative functioning.

Educational Background

Dr. Shivanagowda holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from Visvesvaraya Technological University (VTU), Belagavi, awarded in 2019 for his research in Personalized Learning Systems. Before beginning his doctoral journey at VTU, he had completed two coursework modules and a contact program for Ph.D. scholars at IIT Delhi between 2009 and 2010, which laid a strong foundation for his later research. He earned his Master of Technology (M.Tech.) in Computer Science and Engineering from VTU, Belagavi.

Research & Teaching

Dr. Shivanagowda specializes in Personalized Learning Systems, Human-Computer Interaction, Automated Software Engineering, and Engineering Education. His research has resulted in many publications, a patent on Learning and Mentoring Systems, and a funded project from VTU under the Competitive Research Grant scheme.

Research Profile

He has taught 45 courses (27 of them distinct) across undergraduate and postgraduate programs, consistently experimenting with innovative pedagogical methods such as the Open Assessment Methodology (OAM), CAMP model question papers, and industry-integrated project practices. His teaching philosophy emphasizes exploring multiple learning paths for each course and stitching them together with diverse learner strategies, ensuring that students with different abilities and interests can achieve meaningful learning outcomes.
